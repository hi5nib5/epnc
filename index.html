Battery Storage Solutions

Electrical power needs captured energy to meet the challenges of the future. Grid instability caused by intermittent renewable generation means that captured energy in the form of batteries will form the basis of power grid stability, reduce costs and generate innovative income streams in the future.

EPNC Energy has the knowledge and experience of the battery storage market to advise its customers on how to proceed in a cost-effective way.

The benefit to your business in six paragraphs.

EPNC Energy provides battery storage solutions. We provide a customer focussed approach that develops innovative battery storage solutions which offer market leading benefits to your business. The team specialise in the following:

Reducing costs by

  • Using battery storage to optimise power take from the grid
  • Reduced grid connection charges
  • Reduced TRIAD charges and/or red zone charges
  • Manage power flows to optimise the most cost-effective way to take power

Improving resilience of supply by

  • Co-location of batteries at sites with capacity constraints
  • Co-location of batteries at critical or low reliability sites
  • Strengthen assets or optimise their loading
  • Improving voltage profiles to deliver additional capacity

Storing renewable generation by

  • Using battery storage technology to optimise power flows when the grid becomes unbalanced through mismatches between excess demand or high generation

Generating income by

  • Understanding what additional benefits exist for your sites and / or portfolio

Reducing the carbon footprint by

  • Storing renewable generation
  • Use green technology and store excess capacity
  • Manage power flows to optimise the most cost-effective way to take power

Who are we?

Edgar Goddard

Edgar has wide experience of working in the electricity sector. As an executive and senior manager  within large infrastructure companies he has been responsible for policy development and delivery across world class energy companies.

He has developed and delivered significant infrastructure investments as well as initiating, defining and directing process improvement resulting in improved company performance.

His previous experience covers electricity market operation, the development of commercial and market frameworks, construction, programme management, operations, business transformation and process change, asset management and information services.

Edgar’s experience and achievements have been against a backdrop of fast-moving development in regulation, the industry, the market and the companies themselves.

Edgar entered the energy sector after 10 years as an Engineering Officer in the Royal Navy Submarine Service.

Euan Clifford

Euan has worked in the rail sector for many years and has a deep understanding of the issues the industry faces. As a senior manager he has been responsible for commercial strategies, leading multi-million pound negotiations and delivering programmes of work.

He has delivered significant savings in procurement exercises, obtained start up funding for new programmes and delivered a 3.4TWh flexible energy pricing contract.

Euan is a Chartered Manager and he has a wide diversity of skills in programmes, projects, business change, utility management, commercial agreements and procurement exercises.
